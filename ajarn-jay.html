Ajarn Jay

A traditional Sak Yant master based in Saraburi, Thailand. He is located a little under two hours from Bangkok, bordering Ayutthaya, the ancient capital.

Who he is

Ajarn Jay is a traditionalist. He is able to trace his lineage, he knows the rules of Sak Yant deeply, the sacred script and incantations, and the proper way each design is meant to be done. His years and years as a tattooist have sharpened repetition into true mastery. The result is work his many fans genuinely admire.

His draftsmanship, his command of line, is what truly sets him apart. His tattoos are simply better looking than most: cleaner line, better composition, more beautifully rendered on skin. You can see it yourself, in the gallery below.

A sampling of his work.
Hygiene and process

Ajarn Jay's process matches what's required of a licensed tattoo studio in the West: single-use disposable needles, gloves worn throughout, barrier protection on everything he touches during a session.

The homestay

Ajarn Jay also keeps a property in Sao Hai, a short walk from where he works, that he's set up as a place for visiting clients to stay.

Needs your description

The property itself. Nothing online describes this place, this paragraph has to come from you.

Every Sunday, from 8 in the morning to 4 in the afternoon, a market opens on the grounds of Wat Ton Tan, right on the Pa Sak river. Food stalls line the riverbank, there are boat rides on the water, and a local weaving group demonstrates traditional cloth making nearby. It's a working community market, not a tourist setup, and it only runs one day a week.

A full Sak Yant session, especially for larger pieces, can run for hours. Staying overnight means starting early and finishing properly, instead of rushing back to Bangkok the same day.

Sao Hai is roughly two hours from Bangkok by car.

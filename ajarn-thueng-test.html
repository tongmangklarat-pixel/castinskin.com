A Living Tradition, Carried Forward

The designs Ajarn Thueng tattoos come from traditions passed down through his teachers, and their teachers before them. Many are older forms, preserved within specific lineages and practiced much as they have been for generations.

If you've mostly seen Sak Yant through the designs most commonly sought by visitors to Thailand, the work here may look different. That's part of what makes it distinctive. These designs have stayed rooted in the traditions they came from, rather than changing with the fashions of the modern tattoo world.

Who He Is

Ajarn Thueng

Ajarn Thueng was ordained as a monk and studied for years under Luang Por Prathueang Atikkanto of Wat Nong Yang Thoi in Phetchabun, a monk renowned for his invulnerability magic and his tattooing. After his teacher's death, he went on to study under several more monks and lay masters, learning specific protective, love, and fortune designs from each.

The knowledge also runs in his family. His great-grandfather studied under masters active during the Second World War era. That knowledge passed down to Ajarn Thueng's grandfather, a folk healer, who later passed it to Ajarn Thueng himself.

Ajarn Thueng tattoos using the khem pha pak (เข็มผ่าปาก), an older hand-poke needle style, and works with designs preserved through his teachers' traditions, including some of the original, older forms of patterns that have since changed elsewhere.

He speaks of his work as carrying the tradition forward rather than reinventing it.

The Power of Sak Yant

Magic

Believers since ancient times have held that these tattoos bring protection from accidents, weapons, and harm; invulnerability; escape from danger; victory over rivals and competitors; love and attraction; favor with superiors and people in power; and success in business and trade, among others.

Rituals and Ceremonies

Beyond the tattoo

Alongside tattooing, Ajarn Thueng performs khrop sian, a head-covering blessing meant to bring luck and protection to those who receive it. He also offers sak nam man, tattoos done in oil rather than ink. They leave no visible mark, done purely for the belief behind them rather than for how they look.

Amulets

Made and consecrated

Like many Thai tattoo masters, Ajarn Thueng also makes and consecrates amulets, in the same tradition as his tattoo work. His years of skill as an artist and as a practitioner of the tradition carry into this side of what he does as well.

Community

Ajarn Thueng's samnak draws more than people seeking tattoos. Disciples return for recurring ceremonies, including a monthly full moon holy water blessing and an annual wai khru ceremony each August, and for an ongoing volunteer initiative among his students known as Sid Khongthon Volunteer. Past efforts have repaired a rural school library in Phetchabun, supplied a school in Ratchaburi with equipment, meals, and scholarships, and funded new restrooms for students in need. During Thailand's COVID-19 lockdowns, disciples from Thailand, China, and Singapore stocked a shared pantry outside the samnak for anyone affected by it.

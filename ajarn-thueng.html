Ajarn Thueng.
Traditional Sak Yant tattoos in Bangkok.

Sak Yant tattoos are traditional blessings inscribed into the skin by Ajarns (sacred tattoo masters), inside a samnak (a master's sanctuary).

These are magic spells cast into skin.

What they bring

Protection from accidents, weapons, and harm. Invulnerability. Escape from danger. Victory over rivals and competitors. Love and attraction. Favor with superiors and people in power. Success in business and trade. A turn in fortune during a difficult year. Protection from black magic and evil spirits.

Translator provided

The overwhelming majority of Ajarn Thueng's disciples are Thai. From time to time, he has also worked with disciples from elsewhere, the United States, Canada, France, and Taiwan among them. A translator is provided, highly fluent in English, so nothing is lost or mixed up in the process.

Who he is

Disciple, not customer.

In the Sak Yant tradition, receiving a tattoo makes you Ajarn Thueng's disciple, not simply his customer. He tattoos by hand, using the traditional split-mouth needle rather than a machine, in designs closer to what the tradition looked like generations ago than to what's common now.

He was ordained as a monk and studied for years under Luang Por Prathueang Atikkanto of Wat Nong Yang Thoi in Phetchabun, a monk renowned for his invulnerability magic and his tattooing. After his teacher's death, he went on to study under several more monks and lay masters, learning specific protective, love, and fortune designs from each.

The knowledge also runs in his family. His great-grandfather studied under masters active during the Second World War era. That knowledge passed down to Ajarn Thueng's grandfather, a folk healer, who later passed it to Ajarn Thueng himself.
